Leave Application Concept

A fast and fun way for employees to manage their leaves.

A long time ago in a galaxy far far away (Background/ Challenge/ Brief)

Employees in organisations ‘have to’ use certain services to carry out routine tasks like applying for leaves, viewing their payslips, declaring their investments and so on. Because employees don’t have a choice, the user experience of apps which are carriers of these services is largely ignored, dull and painful.

We decided to take leave management as an area and do a concept piece on it to try and make the experience more efficient and less boring. We examined the flow around applying for a sick or planned leave, modifying and deleting a previously applied for leave.

If the user is not well, they can just push a button and have to do nothing else. If they are applying for a planned (annual) leave they can optionally choose the type of leave, whether it is a full day, first or second half leave, set the dates and may give a reason. In this view they can also see other days where they have applied for or availed leaves and days where they shouldn’t plan a leave because several team members are already on leave.

In the manage leaves view, users can choose the type of leave or absence they would like to manage - by category (sick, annual etc.) and status (planned, availed). They can view their leave balance if appropriate and the history of leaves approved/availed, rejected or pending approval. Selecting a particular leave request opens the ‘edit leave’ view which is similar to the ‘apply for leave’ view.

Primary Users: Employees in organisations.

Key Goals Addressed: Applying for and managing leaves.
