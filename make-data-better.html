Make Data Better Curate

The first step towards managing enterprise data is knowing what you have.

A long time ago in a galaxy far far away (Background/ Challenge/ Brief)

Large firms have a lot of data, and can’t manage what they don’t know they have. A lot of its data isn’t organised but can be into an inventory. The inventory should be accurate, based on real data and complete. Inventories can be of physical or logical assets - the latter are harder but more useful to catalog. Such a catalog or inventory is an organised asset that would make life easier for a large firm.

A key first step is to connect enterprise wide data, which is a non-trivial undertaking for large enterprises tens of thousands of applications and hundreds of millions of data columns. In the connection process there are stages like remote scanning, clustering and concept mapping. App owners or teams responsible for these connections need to know if there is an issue at any step of the process.

Once connections are successfully completed, its time to define, import, improve or maintain the inventory of logical data assets or ‘concepts’. Sometimes users can start working with a preloaded set of concepts which are generic like name or email. Preloaded concepts can also be for a specific use case - like a regulation. Firms may use multiple tools together, so importing glossaries from another tool may be a starting point. An approach while starting from scratch is to model the workflow of the line of business or business entity so that key stakeholders and their interactions become apparent. Looking at nouns etc. in these interactions also gives us a list of concepts to curate.

The act of curation involves training the machine algorithm to better understand and recognise concepts which are important to the business. Once the user creates a concept, the system recommends data points which it believes represent the given concept. Every time the user confirms such a recommendation as being correct or incorrect, the system gets smarter. Within minutes of effort, the system starts to recognise concepts of interest with the desired level of accuracy. It does this for real data coming from diverse sources across a large enterprise. Repeating this process for different concepts results in the robust inventory that enterprises need.

Primary Users
Application Owners, Information Architects

Key Goals Addressed
Connect to enterprise wide data sources, facilitate their automated scanning, create and maintain an accurate inventory of logical data assets in the enterprise.
